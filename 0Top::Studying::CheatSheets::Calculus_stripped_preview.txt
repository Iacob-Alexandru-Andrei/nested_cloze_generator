    An {{c5::{{c3::improper}}}} integral is an integral with one or more {{c4::{{c1::infinite}}}} limits and/or {{c4::{{c2::discontinuous integrands}}}}.&nbsp;

============================================================

  

    An integral is called {{c5::convergent}} if the limit {{c1::exists}} and has a {{c2::finite}} value and {{c5::divergent}} if the limit {{c3::doesn't}} exist or has {{c4::infinite}} value.

============================================================

  

    <br>1. {{c5::{{c1::\(\int_{a}^{\infty}\)}}}} \(f(x)\) \(d x\)={{c4::{{c2::\(\lim _{t \rightarrow \infty}\)}}}} {{c4::{{c3::\(\int_{a}^{t}\)}}}} \(f(x)\) \(d x\)

============================================================

  

    2. {{c5::{{c1::\(\int_{-\infty}^{b}\)}}}} \(f(x)\) \(d x\)={{c4::{{c2::\(\lim _{t \rightarrow-\infty}\)}}}} {{c4::{{c3::\(\int_{t}^{b}\)}}}} \(f(x)\) \(d x\)

============================================================

  

    <br>3. {{c1::\(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\)}} \(f(x) d x\)={{c2::\(\int_{-\infty}^{c}\)}} \(f(x)\) {{c2::\(d x\)}}{{c5::\(+\)}}{{c3::\(\int_{c}^{\infty}\)}} \(f(x)\) {{c3::\(d x\)}} provided both integrals are {{c4::convergent}}.

============================================================

  

    Discontinuity at&nbsp; {{c4::a}}: {{c1::\(\int_a\)}} \(f(x)\) \(d x\)={{c5::{{c2::\(\lim _{t \rightarrow a^{+} }\)}}}} {{c5::{{c3::\(\int_t\)}}}} \(f(x)\) \(d x\)

============================================================

  

    &nbsp;Discontinuity at&nbsp; {{c4::b}}: {{c1::\(\int_a^b\)}} \(f(x) d x\)={{c5::{{c2::\(\lim _{t \rightarrow b^{-} }\)}}}} {{c5::{{c3::\(\int_a^t\)}}}} \(f(x) d x\)

============================================================

  

    Discontinuity at&nbsp;{{c5::\(a&lt;c&lt;b\)}}: <br><ol><li>{{c1::\(\int_a^b\)}} \(f(x) d x\)={{c2::\(\int_a^c\)}} \(f(x) d x\)+{{c3::\(\int_c^b\)}} \(f(x) d x\)&nbsp;</li><li>provided both are {{c4::convergent}}</li></ol>
